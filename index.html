Context

Corporate travel, real friction

VOLL is a corporate travel platform serving companies across Brazil. After nearly 2 years since launching the air ticket product, feedback and operational data revealed critical usability problems costing the business.

Challenge & Goals

Three pillars to redesign

Less friction

Users selecting flights without knowing the total cost until the end of the flow.

Better backend

System couldn't map all round-trip fares during search — reducing options and increasing load time.

Market alignment

Language and interface didn't match modern airline booking tools users were already accustomed to.

Discovery

The previous experience

Solution

Final design

Research

At least 15% chose offline support

The experience generated enough confusion that at least 15% of users abandoned the platform and resorted to calling support — directly costing the business.

Testing

Moderated + Maze testing

Moderated sessions via Google Meet with 5 participants, plus async tests via Maze. Tasks covered flight search, outbound selection, return selection, and fare comparison.

Case Study · 2021–2024

Doris Widget

Increasing feature adoption for a virtual try-on tool

Role
Lead Product Designer
Timeline
3 years
Platform
Mobile · E-commerce
Company
Doris
Context

A Brazilian startup rethinking how people shop online

Doris is a Brazilian startup focused on retail innovation — allowing customers to virtually try on e-commerce clothes using user-centered design. The product lives as a widget embedded in the product page of partner stores like Reserva.

The problem

Only 1.2% completed the task after launch

User tests and interviews couldn't predict that users' real-world contexts — time and place — would be a significant barrier to taking the two required full-body photos. After launch, the data confirmed the gap: activation was happening, but completion wasn't.

Objective

From 1.2% to at least 5% — without breaking revenue

The goal was to research, run squad workshops to form strong hypotheses, then design simple, fast tests to increase main flow usage from 1.2% to at least 5% — without compromising, and possibly increasing, the revenue stream.

Process

Three-phase approach: Empathise → Conceptualise → Design

1. Empathise

No access to real users — so we used our own data

Without direct access to users at this stage, we analyzed existing data to extract insights: main drop-off moments, screen recordings, past user tests, and competitive benchmarking.

2. Conceptualise

Hypotheses, affinity mapping, and CAD matrix

We ran workshops to cluster insights and build a CAD matrix (certainties, assumptions, doubts) across every aspect of the flow. Core issues revealed: lack of awareness of previous value, poor onboarding, misinteractions, and hidden flows.

3. Design

4 fast changes as a multivariable test

Instead of a full redesign, we implemented 4 targeted changes as a multivariable test to accelerate data collection: removed the non-functional onboarding screen, revised the main flow invitation to reduce cognitive confusion, and enhanced the camera UI activation.

Outcomes

200% conversion lift. 285% feature adoption growth.
